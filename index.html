// about

I started in embedded firmware tracing a mwifiex endianness bug on an NXP i.MX platform at Volansys — six hours of kernel log analysis, one le16_to_cpu() call, production-ready on the first re-flash. That moment convinced me the firmware–hardware interface is where the most interesting problems live.

At CableLabs I moved up the stack: profiling 802.11ac/ax EDCA parameters with a Python PCAP pipeline, cutting p95 inter-transmission time by 35% without touching the driver. Now I'm working on IVI infrastructure and Wi-Fi bring-up at Ford Greenfield Labs while building containerized autograding tools for embedded systems education at CU Boulder.
