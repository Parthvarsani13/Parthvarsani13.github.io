About

I got into embedded systems the way most firmware engineers do — by getting my hands on real hardware and refusing to let it stay broken. At Volansys, fixing an endianness bug buried in a Wi-Fi driver's channel-scan path meant tracing corrupted descriptors through the kernel with GDB and ftrace until the root cause gave way to a two-line fix. That experience — sitting at the boundary between silicon, firmware, and the Linux kernel — is what pulled me toward Wi-Fi driver work specifically, and it's the thread that runs through everything on this page: bring-up, debugging, and the discipline of proving a system behaves the way you claim it does.
